Narrative Glitches 

-  Abrupt Scene Transitions: Dream storylines often cut and jump like a broken film.

Without any buildup or reason, the setting and situation change instantaneously. For 
example, you could be in the middle of a dream conversation at home and suddenly find 
yourself at work or in a strange new place with entirely different people. These abrupt 
scene shifts happen with no explanation and you typically feel no shock – the dream 
simply continues in the new setting . Continuity (the in-between steps) is absent, yet the 
dreamer usually isn’t alarmed by the discontinuity. 

-  Bizarre or Illogical Events: Dreams are notorious for “broken” logic. Cause and effect
don’t line up, and impossible events are treated as normal. You might witness absurd 
scenarios – animals talking, objects behaving with a will of their own, nonsensical plot 
developments – and accept them fully while dreaming. Everyday realities can flip (you 
find yourself navigating a building that keeps changing, or doing a job you’ve never had, 
etc.). The dream narrative may also rewrite itself on the fly (a character might suddenly 
claim “actually, this is a movie” or a bad situation inexplicably resolves itself). In short, 
dreams often lack coherent narrative rules: they freely mix settings, invent new plot 
points, and break logical rules of our waking world . Despite the nonsense, it all feels 
strangely natural until you wake up.
