# Sensory Distortions

> *From: Comprehensive Taxonomy of Dream Phenomena*

[📚 Phenomena Index](README.md) | [Spatial and Temporal Anomalies →](spatial-and-temporal-anomalies.md)

---

-  Blurry or Unstable Vision: Dream visuals can be unfocused or prone to sudden change.
For example, text in a dream may blur or letters rearrange each time you try to read it . 
Fine details often refuse to stay consistent, making the scene feel fluid or fuzzy. 

-  Unstable Text and Clocks: Written words and numbers in dreams are notoriously

unreliable. A clock’s time might change from one glance to the next , and lines of text 
may shift or become gibberish upon re-reading . Dreamers often know what something 
says without actually “seeing” stable text. 

-  Heavy or Slow Motion Body: Physical actions can feel impeded. You may run or fight
with extreme effort but move in slow motion or as if through an invisible barrier . This 
common experience of sluggish, heavy limbs can be distressing when trying to escape 
danger in the dream. 

-

Impaired Voice: Similarly, many people find they cannot speak or scream in a dream. 
You might try to shout for help and only a whisper comes out, or no sound at all. This 
sensory failure is often reported in nightmares, adding to a sense of powerlessness 
(even though no physical noise is made during sleep).
