# Spatial and Temporal Anomalies

> *From: Comprehensive Taxonomy of Dream Phenomena*

[📚 Phenomena Index](README.md) | [← Sensory Distortions](sensory-distortions.md) | [Identity Shifts →](identity-shifts.md)

---

-  Shifting or Impossible Landscapes: Dream settings often defy real-world logic. You might

start out in your house and, without transition, it becomes a different place entirely. 
Environments can be surreal or non-Euclidean – for instance, an ordinary room might 
open into an endless maze, or you find yourself in alien, impossible architecture that 
could not exist in reality . Teleportation or location jumps are accepted as normal within 
the dream. 

-  Defiance of Physics: The laws of nature are malleable in dreams. Gravity may loosen or
disappear – allowing you to float or fly at will – and solid objects can behave strangely 
(walls may bend or you might walk through a door that wasn’t there). Cause and effect 
can be inconsistent: you might, for example, drop an object and watch it fall upward or 
transform into something else. These physical absurdities are often embraced with little 

surprise in the dream state. 

-  Time Distortions: Dreams frequently warp the sense of time. A dream can feel as if it

lasted hours or years, yet on waking only a few minutes have passed . Entire lifetimes or 
long adventures might be experienced in a short real-time nap. Sequences of events can 
also shuffle – you might find the ending of a story happening before the beginning, or 
time periods (past, present, future) blending together without logic. 

-  Scene Merging and Loops: Spatial continuity in dreams is tenuous. You may walk down
a familiar street that seamlessly turns into another country. Rooms and locations can 
loop back on themselves (an endless hallway or a door that leads you back to the same 
place). It’s common to encounter the sensation of being lost in a place that keeps 
changing. These geographic anomalies create a dream world with its own fluid topology, 
unconstrained by real geography or linear travel.
